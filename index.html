Part B — Design Brief for Designer
🎯
Project Description
( (I have done some researched and found the flash card device that is launched by Monkey and Bananas ) Electronic Vietnamese Alphabet Poster — a screen-free, visually rich learning tool designed to help heritage learners connect with their mother tongue in a modern Western home environment.
👶
Target Audience
Vietnamese-Chinese children aged 3–7 years old living overseas (heritage learners). Many speak Vietnamese at home but are not yet literate, or are just beginning to learn their mother tongue outside a Vietnamese-dominant environment.
✏️
Design Style
Modern, clean, and child-friendly. The aesthetic should feel warm and approachable rather than overly digital or rigid. A hand-drawn or soft illustrative style works best to create a sense of familiarity and comfort for young children.
🖌️
Illustration Style
Whimsical, friendly characters with simple shapes and expressive faces. Illustrations should reflect Southern Vietnamese culture (e.g., bánh mì for B, đi for D showing a child walking). Avoid generic or Northern-centric imagery.
🎨
Colour Tone
Soft, pastel-based palette with occasional pops of brighter colour for interactive elements. Clean background so letters and illustrations stand out clearly.

Layout Ideas
  • Each letter in its own clearly defined section — a rounded square or circular frame — making the layout easy to navigate.
  • Letters prominently displayed in a bold, easy-to-read font.
  • Each letter paired with a corresponding illustration + the Vietnamese word (Southern dialect) underneath.

Inspiration
Draw inspiration from modern Western educational products such as Melissa & Doug or Little People — where simplicity, friendly character design, and clarity create an engaging learning experience for young children.
🎯
Goal
To create a visually appealing, culturally authentic learning tool that helps overseas Vietnamese children connect with their heritage language in a way that feels natural and fits within a modern Western home environment.
Do's & Don'ts
Do ✅
✅Use Southern Vietnamese spelling for all vocabulary (e.g. "bánh mì" not "bánh mỳ")
✅Illustrations with joyful, approachable facial expressions
✅Clear layout per tile: BIG LETTER + illustration + Vietnamese word
✅Use fonts with full diacritics support (sắc, huyền, nặng, hỏi, ngã)
✅Vary colours across tiles to avoid monotony
Don't ❌
❌Avoid Northern-centric imagery (e.g. áo dài Bắc, bún bò Hà Nội) for a Southern dialect poster
❌No overly saturated or neon colours — must fit a Western home aesthetic
❌Max 2 typefaces — don't mix too many fonts
❌No type below 18pt for word labels — children aged 3–7 need legibility
❌Illustrations only — no real-life photography
